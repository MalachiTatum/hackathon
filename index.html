Welcome to the GridIron Hackathon v2.4.1


The scenario


Your team of advanced engineering ninjas is tasked with helping ensure the future of our mystery site is bright. In order to keep the site from being corrupted with operational drift and technical debt we need to create a development process that eliminates these evils and keeps them from creeping back in. At the end of the event you will have an opportunity to showcase your DevOps skills in front of our esteemed judges. Please review all aspects of the criteria closely, and feel free to focus on a few DevOps goals, or achieve them all. Improvements made to the mystery site itself will be appreciated but not weighted as heavily as the described criteria.


Presenting


This is your time to shine. Teams will have only 5* minutes to showcase their DevOps mastery. There is no required format for the presentation, but it is recommended you submit a code repo with additional documentation that provide detail of the solution’s inner workings. Code repositories will be further scrutinized after the presentation, so be sure to point out any artifacts the judges should pay particular attention to.

Remember this is a DevOps competition, and showcasing how your solution provides value to some common use cases would be useful. For example, what happens after a developer commits new code? When would that code make it to production? What happens if one of your servers mysteriously goes down? What kinds of bugs would get caught before they made it to production? How does the team know the current load on the servers? Where do developers go to start to debug issues in production? How is persistent data managed?

* An additional 3 minutes will be added to teams who decide to create their own web application to explain their application’s purpose.


Scoring Criteria


  • The judges will be looking for the following from your solutions:

  • Do you have a Continuous Integration mechanism?

  • Do you have the capability to achieve Continuous Delivery, if you wanted to?

  • Does your solution use Containerization technology and how will it make the development better?

  • What Automated Testing does your process do before the code is moved to production?

  • What is being Monitored and how would people be alerted of potential errors?

  • How are the Logs Managed?

  • How will the team Communicate ideas, tasks, schedules, versions, and catastrophic errors?

  • How well does your system react to the Chaos of real world environments?

If some of these things are not familiar or too vague please do not hesitate to ask around one of the many SME’s walking by. They can point you to a few resources and answer any questions to get you going back on the keyboard.

Also, it is no big deal if you cannot get to them all. Your goal should be to impress the judges with your DevOps skills, not how fast you can type. Ensuring all the different pieces of your solution work together will win you big points; after all, this is a DevOps competition.

Mystery Web Application Replacement Clause:


If the provided mystery application does not fulfill your hopes and dreams, your team may develop a new application that better suits your solution’s vision. The criterias for this application are as follows:

  • It must be accessible from a modern web browser

  • It must be hosted on the provided AWS environments

  • It must attempt to solve a real world problem

Obviously these criteria are vague, and it will be up to the judge’s discretion to determine the validity of your application during the presentation stage. The judges really like the mystery application, so by using that you will automatically score full marks for that area of scoring criteria.
